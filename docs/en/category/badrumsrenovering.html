Bathroom renovation

20 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 20 common questions and answers in the category bathroom renovation for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What does a bathroom renovation cost?

Full renovation of a 4-6 m² bathroom: 150,000-400,000 SEK (2026). Labour 50-70%. Stone and premium fixtures increase the cost. ROT applies to the labour. Zaragoza AB average in Helsingborg: 200,000 SEK.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

How long does a bathroom renovation take?

3-6 weeks for a full renovation including waterproofing membrane, tiling, plumbing, electrical and ventilation. Drying time for the membrane may extend this. Zaragoza AB delivers turnkey bathrooms with Säker Vatten certification.

Sources: Säker Vatten

Is a permit needed for bathroom renovation?

Normally not, if within existing surfaces. Relocating water outlets or drains requires a plumbing notification to the municipality. New wet rooms in a previously dry space: contact the building committee.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. · BBR 6

What is a wet room certificate?

A certificate that the work was carried out in accordance with industry rules (BBV — the Swedish Tile Council's industry rules, or Säker Vatten). Required for insurance claims; also makes a sale easier.

Sources: BKR · Säker Vatten

What is the waterproofing membrane in a bathroom?

A watertight layer beneath the tile/clinker that prevents water from reaching the load-bearing structure. Must be BBV-approved for Swedish bathrooms. An incorrect membrane means insurance will not pay for damage.

Sources: BBV

How often should I replace the bathroom?

Modern wet rooms 20-30 years with good maintenance. Signs of needed replacement: discoloured membrane, leaks, damp patches in adjacent rooms. Zaragoza AB offers a free wet-room inspection.

Sources: Branschpraxis

Does a bathroom renovation qualify for ROT?

Yes, all labour costs qualify for ROT (demolition, plumbing, electrical, tiling, membrane, fixture installation). Materials are not ROT-eligible. Zaragoza AB splits the invoice correctly.

Sources: Inkomstskattelagen 67 kap.

Should I have a bathtub or a shower?

Shower: less space, lower water consumption, modern preference. Bathtub: families with small children, valued at sale. A combination (bathtub with shower function) is the classic compromise.

Sources: Branschdata

Must I have mechanical ventilation in the bathroom?

Yes, under BBR ch. 6. An extract fan with a minimum 15 l/s continuous flow, or 30 l/s during showering. Preferably controlled by humidity or presence. FTX (heat-recovery) unit is best.

Sources: BBR 6

What is underfloor heating in a bathroom?

Electric or water-based heating cable/mat under the tiles. Standard in modern Swedish bathrooms. Comfortable, reduces humidity, quick-drying floor. Installed by a certified electrician/plumber. ROT-eligible.

Sources: BBR 9

Which toilet should I choose?

Standard floor-mounted: Duravit, Gustavsberg, Ifö. Wall-hung (modern, easy to clean): requires a built-in cistern. Shower-WC (electric): comfort but more expensive. Eco models with dual flush save water.

Sources: Materialjämförelse

Can I renovate the bathroom in a tenant-owned apartment (bostadsrätt)?

Yes, with the association's approval. The bylaws determine the limits: normally you may renovate within the surface finishes but not change pipes/electrical panel without permission. A wet-room certificate is required by many associations.

Sources: Bostadsrättslagen

What are the BBR requirements for bathrooms?

BBR 6: waterproofing solutions per industry rules, mechanical ventilation of at least 15 l/s, slope conditions towards the floor drain, safety zones for electrical equipment, accessibility (in new construction of multi-dwelling buildings).

Sources: BBR 6

How large should the floor drain be?

Recommended 150×150 mm (minimum) for the main drain. For wheelchair access and good drainage: 400×400 mm linear drain. Per BBR, the slope towards the drain should be 1:50 to 1:80 on the floor.

Sources: BBR 6

What electrical installation rules apply in a bathroom?

Strict zoning (zones 0-3) governs where electrical equipment may be placed. Only a qualified electrician may work on it. RCD 30 mA residual-current device is mandatory. Elsäkerhetsverket (Swedish Electrical Safety Board) supervises.

Sources: ELSÄK-FS

What does only retiling a bathroom cost?

About 1,500-3,500 SEK/m² including removal of old tiles, new membrane, grouting and slope work. A 5 m² bathroom: approx. 80,000-150,000 SEK (2026).

Sources: Branschindex 2026

Which waterproofing method is best?

Membrane systems from established manufacturers (Weber, Kiilto, Schönox) with foil or liquid membrane. All must be BBV-approved. Correct execution matters more than brand.

Sources: BBV

How long a warranty should I get on a bathroom renovation?

Industry standard: 2 years on workmanship under the Consumer Services Act. However, hidden defects may be complained about for up to 10 years under ABS 18. Zaragoza AB gives a 5-year extended workmanship warranty on wet rooms.

Sources: ABS18 · Konsumenttjänstlagen

What are 'made-to-measure modules' in a bathroom?

An alternative to on-site construction: prefabricated wall modules with integrated membrane and tiling. Faster installation (days instead of weeks). They must however be BBV-approved to meet Swedish standards.

Sources: BBV

What do I do if I discover moisture damage during renovation?

Stop the work, document it (photos), contact the insurance company for a moisture damage claim. The renovation may become more expensive. Zaragoza AB handles moisture remediation in cooperation with a certified moisture inspector.

Sources: Svensk Fuktkontroll
