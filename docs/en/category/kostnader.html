Costs & quotes

15 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 15 common questions and answers in the category costs & quotes for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What does a new-build house cost?

Turnkey 130 m² villa in Skåne: 4–6 MSEK (2026) including land. Construction cost: 25,000–40,000 SEK/m². Land cost varies greatly by municipality.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

How do I compare quotes?

Request a labour/materials breakdown, ask all firms for the same specification, check the ÄTA policy, verify each firm's legitimacy (all 9 steps). The lowest price is not always the best.

Sources: Branschpraxis

How much more than the quote will the project end up costing?

On average 10–25% over the original quote due to ÄTA and surprises. Budget a 15–20% margin. Detailed quotes with a clear specification reduce surprises.

Sources: Branschdata

What is fixed price vs running account (löpande räkning)?

Fixed price: the contractor bears the risk, price-certain for the client. Running account: actual hours × hourly rate, risk for the client but flexible. ABS18 recommends fixed price for private individuals.

Sources: ABS18

What hourly rate is reasonable for builders?

Carpenter: 550–800 SEK/h. Plumber: 700–950 SEK/h. Electrician: 700–900 SEK/h. Prices vary by region and firm. Extra for weekend work. Specialists can cost more.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

Can I negotiate the price?

Yes, often 5–15% is possible. Ask about alternative materials, simpler solutions, a different schedule. Never squeeze the timeline without sacrificing quality. Zaragoza AB is open to dialogue.

Sources: Branschpraxis

What is the SEK/m² cost for renovation?

Kitchen: 10,000–30,000 SEK/m². Bathroom: 30,000–80,000 SEK/m². Extension: 18,000–32,000 SEK/m². Facade: 600–2,200 SEK/m². Roof: 600–2,500 SEK/m². ROT on labour gives a 30% discount.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

Can I get a loan for renovation?

Yes, a mortgage on the encumbered property or a separate renovation loan. The bank will want a quote, building permit (if relevant), project description. ROT reduces the effective borrowing need.

Sources: Bankbranschen

How much does renovation raise my home's value?

Kitchen + bathroom: 80–120% of the investment is recovered at sale. Roof replacement: 60–80%. Full new-look styling: 50–70%. ROT-eligible work cuts the net cost.

Sources: Hemnet Analys

What do building permit + design cost?

Building permit: 25,000–50,000 SEK for a single-family house. Architect/design: 50,000–200,000 SEK depending on scope. Control officer (KA): 15,000–40,000 SEK. Total 5–10% of construction cost.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

How do I calculate the ROT discount in my budget?

Take labour cost × 30% = your discount, capped at 50,000/person/year. Example: 200,000 SEK labour × 30% = 60,000 SEK — capped at 50,000 SEK for one person. Materials are 100% without discount.

Sources: Skatteverket

Why are prices higher in big cities?

Wages, premises and materials are more expensive. Skåne (Helsingborg, Malmö, Lund) runs 5–15% above the Swedish average. Rural areas 10–20% lower. Zaragoza AB operates in Skåne at market rates.

Sources: SCB

What are the hidden costs in a construction project?

Construction waste handling, building permit fees, architect, control officer, site surveying, electrical connections, temporary housing (for larger renovations), insurance, VAT on self-ordered materials.

Sources: Branschpraxis

Should I pick the cheapest quote?

Not always. A very low price may indicate: inferior materials, undeclared labour, unqualified work, planned ÄTA. Choose based on verification + legitimacy + price — not price alone.

Sources: Konsumentverket

How do I stay within budget?

Detailed specification, limit ÄTA, written documentation of changes, milestone payments, qualified inspections, legitimate firm. A 15–20% buffer is realistic.

Sources: Branschpraxis
