Inspection (Besiktning)

7 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 7 common questions and answers in the category inspection (besiktning) for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What is a slutbesiktning (final inspection)?

The slutbesiktning is the final inspection of the contract works, in which an independent inspector determines whether the work is performed in accordance with the contract. An approved slutbesiktning means the works are handed over, the guarantee period begins to run, and the final payment falls due under AB 04 chapter 7.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 7 · ABT 06 kap. 7

What is a garantibesiktning (guarantee inspection)?

The garantibesiktning is carried out before the end of the guarantee period, normally after two and five years respectively, to detect defects that have appeared during the guarantee period. The contractor is obliged to rectify the noted defects. The inspection is requested in writing by the client under AB 04 chapter 7 § 3.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 7 § 3

Who appoints the inspector?

In contract works under AB 04/ABT 06 the inspector is appointed by the client, but the parties may agree otherwise. The inspector must be impartial, qualified and independent. In consumer contract works (ABS 18) both parties have the right to be present.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 7 § 6 · ABS 18 p. 19

What is a statusbesiktning (condition survey)?

A statusbesiktning is a visual inspection of a property's condition, often at property transfer. The inspector produces a report with observed defects and proposed measures. It is not a guarantee-giving inspection but an informational basis for the buyer or owner.

Sources: SBR · Boverket

What certification is required for an inspector?

For contract inspection, an SBR-approved inspector or certification via RISE or Kiwa is recommended. For transfer inspections, SBR or BAB certification is often required. Certification imposes requirements on training, experience and liability insurance.

Sources: SBR byggingenjörerna · RISE

What happens if the final inspection is not approved?

If material defects are noted, the inspector may reject the works. The contractor must then rectify the defects and a new inspection is held. If the defects are of minor importance, the inspection may be approved with remarks to be addressed within an agreed time under AB 04 chapter 7 § 12.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 7 § 12

Can I appeal the inspection report?

An inspection report cannot be appealed in the traditional sense, but a dissatisfied party can call for an over-inspection within one month under AB 04 chapter 7 § 8. The over-inspection is carried out by three inspectors and its report replaces the original inspection.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 7 § 8
