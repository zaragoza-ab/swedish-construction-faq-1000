Dispute resolution

9 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 9 common questions and answers in the category dispute resolution for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What do I do in a dispute with a construction firm?

Step 1: written complaint. Step 2: ARN complaint (consumer, free, binding). Step 3: district court. Home insurance may cover legal protection. Zaragoza AB is a member of Byggföretagen's dispute resolution scheme.

Sources: ARN · Konsumentverket

What is ARN?

Allmänna reklamationsnämnden (the National Board for Consumer Disputes). Free dispute resolution for consumers. Decisions are advisory but followed in 80% of cases. Fast (about 6 months). No lawyer required.

Sources: ARN

Can I get legal protection from home insurance?

Yes, most home insurance policies include legal protection of 100,000-300,000 SEK. It applies to disputes over construction projects on your own home. Deductible 25%.

Sources: Försäkringsbranschen

What is a settlement?

An agreement where the parties compromise without a judgment. Faster and cheaper than a trial. A lawyer or mediator can help. A settlement is binding once written and signed.

Sources: Rättegångsbalken

How long do I have to complain?

Under the Consumer Services Act: "within a reasonable time" after discovery, at the latest 3 years after the work was completed. Hidden defects under ABS18: 10 years after final inspection.

Sources: Konsumenttjänstlagen · ABS18

Can I claim damages?

Yes, if you can prove financial loss as a result of the defect (e.g. secondary damage from a water leak). The Tort Liability Act and the contract govern the scope.

Sources: Skadeståndslagen

What does it cost to pursue a construction dispute in court?

Filing fee: 900-2,800 SEK. Lawyer: 2,500-4,000 SEK per hour. A trial typically involves 50,000-200,000 SEK in legal fees. The losing party pays the winner's costs.

Sources: Tingsrätten

What is the Consumer Services Act?

SFS 1985:716. It protects consumers buying services (including construction services). Requirements include professional standards, information, and right to complain. It applies when a private individual hires a business.

Sources: Konsumenttjänstlagen

Can Zaragoza AB mediate in a dispute?

Zaragoza AB works only on its own projects, but in the event of conflict we refer to Byggföretagen's dispute resolution scheme or ARN. Professional dialogue and documentation minimize disputes from the start.

Sources: Zaragoza AB policy
