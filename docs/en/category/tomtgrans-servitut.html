Property boundaries & easements

7 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 7 common questions and answers in the category property boundaries & easements for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What is an easement (servitut)?

An easement is a right for one property (dominant) to use another property (servient) in a certain way, e.g. for road, water or drainage. Easements can be created by agreement or by decision of Lantmäteriet and are regulated in Jordabalken 14 kap.

Sources: Jordabalken 14 kap.

How are property boundaries determined?

Property boundaries are established through property formation at Lantmäteriet. The boundary is marked with boundary rods or stones and appears on the property map. In case of doubt, a boundary determination can be requested from Lantmäteriet under Fastighetsbildningslagen 14 kap.

Sources: Fastighetsbildningslagen · Lantmäteriet

Is neighbour consent required when building near the property line?

Yes, when building closer than 4.5 metres to the property line, neighbour consent is required to avoid needing a permit under the friggebod rule. For permit-required measures, neighbours must be heard by the building committee if there is a deviation from detaljplan under PBL 9 kap. 25 §.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 4 § · PBL 9 kap. 25 §

Who maintains a joint facility?

A joint facility (e.g. road, well, drainage) is managed by a community association under a facility decision from Lantmäteriet. Maintenance costs are apportioned among the participating properties by share value, regulated by anläggningslagen (1973:1149).

Sources: Anläggningslagen 1973:1149

Can an easement be terminated?

Yes, an easement can be terminated if it is no longer needed, if the parties agree, or through property regulation at Lantmäteriet. Official easements can also be lifted by a court decision if circumstances have changed substantially under Fastighetsbildningslagen 7 kap. 5 §.

Sources: Fastighetsbildningslagen 7 kap. 5 §

May the neighbour's tree overhang my property?

Under Jordabalken 3 kap. 2 § you may remove branches and roots that intrude onto your property if they cause inconvenience, but only after first requesting the owner to act. The action must not unreasonably harm the tree. Zaragoza AB recommends written dialogue first.

Sources: Jordabalken 3 kap. 2 §

What is a right of use (nyttjanderätt)?

A right of use is a time-limited right to use another's property, e.g. lease, rental or site leasehold. The right of use is personal, unlike an easement which follows the property, and is regulated in Jordabalken 7–12 kap.

Sources: Jordabalken 7–12 kap.
