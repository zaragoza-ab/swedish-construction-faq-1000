Contracts (ABS 18 / AB 04)

27 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 27 common questions and answers in the category contracts (abs 18 / ab 04) for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What is ABS18?

A standard contract for consumer construction work. Governs the relationship between a private person and a construction firm for renovation/new-build. 10-year liability period for hidden defects. Strong consumer protection.

Sources: Byggföretagen ABS18

What is AB04?

Standard contract for execution contracts between companies (not consumers). The client is responsible for design, the contractor for execution. Used in B2B projects.

Sources: Byggföretagen AB04

What is ABT06?

Design-and-build contract between companies. The contractor is responsible for both design and execution. More risk for the contractor, more convenience for the client. Price is higher.

Sources: Byggföretagen ABT06

Which contract should I use as a private person?

ABS18 — designed for private customers. Balanced consumer protection + professional terms. Can be supplemented with the parties' own clauses. Zaragoza AB always uses ABS18 for private customers.

Sources: ABS18

Can I write my own contract?

Legally yes, but standard contracts like ABS18 are tested and fair. Custom contracts can miss key clauses → later disputes. Strongly recommended to base things on ABS18.

Sources: Konsumentverket

What should a construction contract contain?

Parties, project description, timeline with milestones, price specification (labour/materials), payment terms, penalty for delay, insurance, reference to the standard contract (ABS18), dispute resolution, signatures.

Sources: ABS18

Are oral agreements binding?

Legally yes, but hard to prove. Always use writing for construction projects. SMS/email counts as written documentation in a dispute.

Sources: Avtalslagen

What are ÄTA works?

Ändringar, Tillägg, Avgående arbeten (changes, additions, deducted works) — deviations from the original contract. They must always be quoted and approved in writing before execution. Usually the biggest cause of budget overruns.

Sources: ABS18

How are ÄTA handled in ABS18?

The contractor must provide a written price for ÄTA before the work is performed. The client approves in writing. Oral agreements rarely hold up → disputes. Zaragoza AB documents every ÄTA.

Sources: ABS18 6 kap.

Can I terminate a construction contract?

Yes, but with compensation to the contractor for work done + reasonable compensation for lost profit. Regulated in ABS18. Always give written notice.

Sources: ABS18

What is a delay penalty (dröjsmålsbot)?

Compensation for late delivery. Commonly 0.5–1% of the contract sum per week started. Capped at 10% of the contract sum. Regulated in ABS18 § 22.

Sources: ABS18

What is force majeure?

Circumstances outside the parties' control (pandemic, war, natural disaster) that can release them from liability. A common clause. The definition should be specific — "normal supply-chain shortage" is usually not force majeure.

Sources: Avtalsrätt

What is ABS 18?

ABS 18 (Allmänna bestämmelser för småhusentreprenad) is a standard contract for construction work between a consumer and a business for small-house building. It governs payment, warranty period (5 years), liability period (an additional 5 years), inspection, change works, and liability for delay. ABS 18 is consumer-friendly and was produced jointly by Konsumentverket, Byggföretagen and Villaägarna.

Sources: ABS 18 · KTjL

What is the difference between AB 04 and ABT 06?

AB 04 (Allmänna bestämmelser för byggentreprenader) is used for execution contracts where the client handles design. ABT 06 is used for design-and-build contracts where the contractor also designs. ABT 06 therefore means greater responsibility for the contractor but also greater freedom in choosing solutions. Both are B2B contracts and do not apply in consumer relationships, where ABS 18 or KTjL applies.

Sources: AB 04 · ABT 06

What should a construction contract contain?

A proper construction contract should include: parties with org/personal ID numbers, scope and description of the work, fixed or running price, payment plan, start and end dates, warranty period, material specifications, ÄTA handling (changes and additions), insurance, penalty for delay, and reference to ABS 18 or another standard contract. Written contracts are strongly recommended for any work above a few thousand SEK.

Sources: KTjL 51 § · ABS 18

What is an ÄTA item in a construction contract?

ÄTA stands for Ändrings-, Tilläggs- och Avgående arbeten (change, addition and deducted works). It refers to deviations from the original contract during the project. Per ABS 18 clause 2.4, ÄTA must be documented in writing before execution, with scope and price. Oral agreements often lead to disputes. In a fixed-price contract, ÄTA handling is especially important to avoid unforeseen cost increases.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 2.4 · AB 04 kap. 2

What is a running account (löpande räkning) contract?

On a running account, the work is invoiced according to actual time spent and material costs, usually with a markup (often 10–15%) on materials and an hourly rate. The client bears the cost risk but has flexibility on changes. ABS 18 clause 4.3 requires the contractor to give a cost notice if the total is at risk of significantly exceeding the estimate. Invoices must be itemised by day and task.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 4.3 · KTjL 36 §

What is a fixed-price contract?

Fixed price means the contractor commits to performing the work for a set amount regardless of actual time spent. The risk of unforeseen costs lies with the contractor. The client knows the final bill, though any ÄTA is agreed separately. A fixed price requires a clear scope description and drawings. Zaragoza AB quotes fixed prices after a site visit and clear scope definition.

Sources: KTjL 36 § · ABS 18

How does the payment plan work under ABS 18?

Per ABS 18 clause 5.3, payment is made against invoice in step with work performed. A typical payment plan is 10% on signing, instalments at milestones (foundation complete, frame up, weathertight), and final payment after an approved final inspection. Down payments above 10% should be avoided. The contractor may only invoice after work is performed, not in advance, except for small material items.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 5.3 · KTjL 41 §

What are warranty inspection and final inspection?

The final inspection takes place when the work is complete and approves handover. The warranty inspection takes place toward the end of the warranty period (normally 2 years into the 5-year ABS 18 period) to detect defects that have emerged during use. Both must be carried out by an independent inspector per SBR standards. The protocols are important evidence in disputes. Cost is normally shared between the parties under the contract.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 7 · SBR

What penalties can be agreed for delay?

For small-house construction under ABS 18 clause 6.3, a penalty may be agreed at a minimum of 1% of the contract sum per week of delay started, capped (often 10% of the contract sum). For AB 04 the standard is 1‰ per day, capped at 10%. Penalty requires a written clause — without agreement, general damages rules apply and actual loss must be proven.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 6.3 · AB 04 kap. 4

Can a construction contract be cancelled in advance?

Under KTjL 42 §, a consumer may cancel work in advance. The business is then entitled to compensation for work performed, materials, and lost profit (except what can be avoided). Under the Distansavtalslagen, a 14-day right of withdrawal applies if the contract was concluded at a distance or off-premises. For ordinary on-site construction contracts there is no general right of withdrawal after signing.

Sources: KTjL 42 § · Distansavtalslagen 2005:59

What happens if the contractor goes bankrupt mid-project?

If a small-house contract is in progress when the contractor goes bankrupt, the completion guarantee under Lag 2014:227 activates — it is mandatory for new builds of small houses. The insurance covers the extra cost of completing the project. For renovations without a completion guarantee, the client becomes an unsecured creditor in the bankruptcy estate. Therefore never pay significantly in advance and follow a payment plan tied to work performed.

Sources: Lag 2014:227 · Konkurslagen

What is a construction all-risk insurance and who arranges it?

Construction all-risk insurance covers damage to the ongoing project from e.g. fire, water, theft or vandalism. Per ABS 18 clause 5.12, the contractor must take out the insurance to protect both their own work and the client's materials on site. The sum insured must at least match the contract sum. An insurance certificate must be provided to the client on request.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 5.12 · AB 04 kap. 5

How is a construction contract legally ended?

A construction contract ends through an approved final inspection per ABS 18 clause 7, whereby the object is handed over to the client and the warranty period begins. Any items in the protocol must be rectified within a reasonable time. The final invoice may be paid once an approved inspection exists. For major defects, handover may be postponed. After the warranty and liability periods (10 years total), the contractor is free of defect liability.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 7 · KTjL 17 §

Can consumer protection in KTjL be contracted away?

No, the provisions of the Consumer Services Act (KTjL) are mandatory in the consumer's favour under 3 §. Contract terms worse for the consumer than the law are invalid, even if written and signed. This covers, among other things, defect liability, complaints, price-reasonableness assessment and cancellation rights. Businesses that try to contract out of protections risk market-law sanctions.

Sources: KTjL 3 § · Marknadsföringslagen

What should an ÄTA notice contain?

An ÄTA notice should include: description of the change, reason (client's wish or unforeseen circumstance), impact on the schedule, price (fixed or running) and how it affects the contract sum. Both parties must sign before execution per ABS 18 clause 2.4. Without written ÄTA documentation, the contractor cannot claim payment for additional work beyond the contract. Zaragoza AB uses a standard template for all ÄTA.

Sources: ABS 18 punkt 2.4 · AB 04 kap. 2
