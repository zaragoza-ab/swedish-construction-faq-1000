Roofing

20 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 20 common questions and answers in the category roofing for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What does a roof replacement cost?

Depends on material and size: clay tiles 1,500–2,500 SEK/m², sheet metal 900–1,800 SEK/m², felt/roofing paper 600–1,200 SEK/m² (2026, Skåne). An average 120 m² roof: 180,000–300,000 SEK in total.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

How long does a new roof last?

Clay tiles 40–60 years, concrete tiles 30–50 years, sheet metal 40–70 years, felt/paper 15–30 years. Lifespan depends on maintenance and exposure. Zaragoza AB provides a 10-year warranty on workmanship.

Sources: Materialspecifikationer

Is a building permit needed for a roof replacement?

When replacing with the same roof type and colour: usually no permit. For a changed appearance (different colour or material) a permit is required. In detaljplan areas, roof type rules are often strict.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 2 §

Does a roof replacement qualify for ROT?

Yes, roof replacement qualifies for ROT on labour costs (30%, max 50,000 SEK/person/year). Materials are not ROT-eligible. Zaragoza AB handles ROT directly on the invoice.

Sources: Inkomstskattelagen 67 kap.

What are signs that a roof needs replacing?

Cracked/displaced tiles, moss and lichen, water leaking indoors, visible corrosion on sheet metal, worn roofing felt with blisters. Inspect the roof in spring and autumn.

Sources: Branschpraxis

Can I put solar panels on the roof?

Yes, solar panels often require no permit outside heritage-protected areas. The roof should have good load-bearing capacity and be less than 10 years old or good for 25+ more years. Reverse VAT normally applies for the installer.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 3 c §

What is underlayment?

A watertight layer (often roofing felt or diffusion-open membrane) beneath the visible roof. Critical for leak prevention. All modern roofs have underlayment according to AMA Hus 2022.

Sources: AMA Hus 2022

Must roofers hold a qualification?

No general statutory certification, but Byggföretagen's roofing contractor programme provides a quality mark. Sheet-metal workers may need special qualifications. Zaragoza AB works only with certified roofing specialists.

Sources: Branschpraxis

What roof pitch is best for Swedish climate?

22–45° is standard. Below 14° requires extra sealing (low pitch = standing water). Over 45° is uncommon and costlier. Risk of snow slides at high pitch; ice risk at low pitch.

Sources: Boverket

How do I handle snow safety on the roof?

Snow guards are often required in public locations or on steep slopes. A ladder and roof walkway are recommended for safe work. BBR ch. 8:2 regulates roof safety.

Sources: BBR 8

Should I choose tile or metal?

Tile: classic look, 40–60 year lifespan, quiet, more expensive. Metal: faster installation, 40–70 year lifespan, noisier in rain, cheaper. Both meet BBR. Choice depends on the house, budget and detaljplan.

Sources: Materialjämförelser

How long does a roof replacement take?

100–150 m² roof: 1–2 weeks in good weather. Complex roofs with dormers and cupolas: 3–4 weeks. Zaragoza AB always plans with weather buffer and can deliver turnkey.

Sources: Zaragoza AB erfarenhet

What happens if I find asbestos in the roof?

Asbestos-cement roofs (eternit) were common until 1982. They must be handled by a certified remediation firm. Arbetsmiljöverket requires permits and special equipment. Expensive process, around 50–100% more than ordinary removal.

Sources: AFS 2006:1

Does home insurance cover roof replacement?

Normally not planned replacements. If the roof is destroyed in a storm or damaged by fire: yes. Age deductions apply — a new roof may give pro-rata compensation based on age.

Sources: Konsumenträttsliga nämnden

Should I replace the roof myself?

Not recommended. Roofing is dangerous (fall risk), requires BBR knowledge, and ROT only applies to professional work. A faulty installation can cost more than hiring a professional from the start.

Sources: Arbetsmiljöverket

Can I paint a metal roof instead of replacing it?

Yes, if the metal is structurally intact and only the surface has worn. Roof painting costs 300–500 SEK/m² and extends life 10–15 years. Requires thorough prep. ROT-eligible.

Sources: Branschpraxis

What is a jack rafter and what does it do?

A jack rafter is an angled roof rafter that connects to hip rafters on hipped roofs. It enables complex roof forms. More structurally demanding than ordinary truss construction.

Sources: Träkonstruktion

What insulation level is required in the roof?

BBR 2026: max U-value 0.13 W/m²K (≈ 350 mm mineral wool). Passive house: even better (0.08–0.10). Zaragoza AB insulates according to current BBR plus client preferences.

Sources: BBR 9

Can I replace only part of the roof?

Yes, but often wasteful. New material mixed with old → uneven ageing, full replacement later costs more. If only a small section is damaged (leak, storm damage), spot repair is reasonable.

Sources: Branschpraxis

How does the detaljplan in Helsingborg affect roof choice?

Older Helsingborg blocks often have strict detaljplan rules on tile colour (red, black), pitch, and roof form. The city planning office can quickly answer plan questions. Zaragoza AB has experience with Helsingborg's block rules.

Sources: Helsingborgs stad
