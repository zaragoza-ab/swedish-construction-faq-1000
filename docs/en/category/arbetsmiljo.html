Work environment (AFS)

25 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 25 common questions and answers in the category work environment (afs) for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What is a work environment plan (arbetsmiljöplan)?

A planning document for the work environment on a construction site. Mandatory under AFS 1999:3 for projects exceeding 500 person-working-days or involving particularly hazardous work. Responsibility: the building owner.

Sources: AFS 1999:3

What are BAS-P and BAS-U?

Construction work environment coordinators: P (projektering, design) in the design phase, U (utförande, execution) on site. Can be the same or different persons. Competence requirements per AFS 2008:16.

Sources: AFS 2008:16

What PPE is required on a construction site?

Helmet, safety shoes, visible high-visibility clothing (hi-vis class), gloves. For specific risks: hearing protection, respiratory protection, safety goggles, harness for work at height.

Sources: AFS 2001:3

How long may one work at height without fall protection?

Never without protection above 2 metres. A harness or collective protection (scaffolding, railing) is required. Breaking the rule = sanction fee plus accident risk.

Sources: AFS 2020:1

What is a safety walk (skyddsrond)?

A regular walkthrough in which the BAS-U, safety representative and site supervisor check safety on the construction site. Weekly at minimum. Minutes are recorded with actions and deadlines.

Sources: AFS 1999:3

Must I report incidents?

Yes, all incidents (near-misses) must be reported per AFS 2001:3. Serious ones go directly to Arbetsmiljöverket (Swedish Work Environment Authority). Used to prevent future accidents.

Sources: AFS 2001:3

What is quartz dust?

Dust particles from concrete, brick and render — carcinogenic. Requires water mist/dust extraction when sawing/drilling, FFP3 respiratory protection. Arbetsmiljöverket sanctions inadequate protection.

Sources: AFS 2015:2

Who is responsible for the work environment?

The building owner overall. BAS-P and BAS-U operationally. The employer for their own personnel. Workers also have their own responsibility to follow the rules. A safety representative can stop hazardous work.

Sources: AML · AFS

Can a safety representative stop the work?

Yes, in case of immediate and serious danger. Called the 'right of stoppage' under AML (Work Environment Act) 6 kap. 7 §. Work resumes after the danger has been addressed or Arbetsmiljöverket has decided.

Sources: AML 6 kap.

What is asbestos remediation?

Certified removal of asbestos-containing material (roofs, pipes, insulation). Must be performed by a company authorised by Arbetsmiljöverket. Strict waste handling. Cost 3-5x regular demolition.

Sources: AFS 2006:1

What is AFS 1999:3?

AFS 1999:3 is Arbetsmiljöverket's regulations on building and civil engineering work. It governs the construction work environment coordinators (BAS-P for design and BAS-U for execution), the work environment plan, advance notification to Arbetsmiljöverket for larger projects, and the responsibilities of the building owner, designer and contractor. A fundamental regulation for all construction activity in Sweden.

Sources: AFS 1999:3 · Arbetsmiljölagen 3 kap.

When is a work environment plan required on a construction site?

A work environment plan is required under AFS 1999:3 § 8 when advance notification must be submitted (work lasting more than 30 working days with more than 20 people simultaneously, or more than 500 person-days), or when the work involves particularly risky elements such as roof work over 2 m, work in excavations deeper than 2 m, asbestos or heavy lifts. BAS-P draws up the plan; BAS-U keeps it updated.

Sources: AFS 1999:3 § 8 · Bilaga A

What are BAS-P and BAS-U?

BAS-P (construction work environment coordinator for planning and design) is responsible for work environment matters during design. BAS-U (for execution) coordinates the work environment on site during construction. Both are appointed in writing by the building owner under AFS 1999:3 § 6. The building owner is responsible for ensuring the coordinators have the right competence, experience and resources. Documented training is required.

Sources: AFS 1999:3 § 6 · Arbetsmiljölagen 3 kap. 7a §

When is advance notification to Arbetsmiljöverket required?

Advance notification under AFS 1999:3 § 7 is required when a construction project is expected to last more than 30 working days and employ more than 20 people simultaneously, or when the work involves more than 500 person-days. The building owner is responsible for the notification, submitted via Arbetsmiljöverket's e-service. It must be submitted before construction starts and updated on changes.

Sources: AFS 1999:3 § 7 · Arbetsmiljöverket

What rules apply to roof work?

Roof work above 2 metres requires fall-protection measures under AFS 1981:14 and AFS 1999:3. Options include railings (at least 1.0 m high), scaffolding, safety nets or personal fall-protection equipment with anchorage. Roof safety must be planned before the work begins. Ladders may only be used for short-duration work. A work environment plan is always required for roof work over 2 m.

Sources: AFS 1981:14 · AFS 1999:3 Bilaga A

What does AFS require for asbestos work?

Asbestos work is regulated by AFS 2006:1 and requires a permit from Arbetsmiljöverket for demolition or processing. Personnel must have completed specific training, use respiratory protection of at least P3, and wear disposable coveralls. The work area must be cordoned off and placed under negative pressure. The waste is handled as hazardous waste. Asbestos sampling must be done when in doubt before demolishing material from before 1982.

Sources: AFS 2006:1 · Avfallsförordningen

What requirements apply to scaffolding?

Scaffolding is governed by AFS 2013:4. Scaffolding above 2 metres must be designed, assembled by trained personnel and inspected before use. Railings must have a top rail at 1.0 m, an intermediate rail and a toe board. Training requirements: general knowledge (up to 9 m), specific training (above 9 m or hanging scaffolds). The scaffold is tagged with a sign showing the load class and the assembly manager.

Sources: AFS 2013:4 · SS-EN 12811

May minors work on a construction site?

Under AFS 2012:3 and the Work Environment Act 5 kap., young people under 18 may not perform hazardous work such as roof work over 2 m, work in excavations, asbestos handling or work with heavy machinery. 16-17-year-olds may perform lighter construction tasks under supervision. Under 16, work on a construction site is in principle prohibited, except for lighter school-based practice.

Sources: AFS 2012:3 · Arbetsmiljölagen 5 kap.

What is ID06 and why is it required?

ID06 is an industry system for electronic staff registers and access control on construction sites. Each worker has a personal ID06 card linked to the employer's F-tax and organisation number. The system supports Skatteverket's staff register requirements under Skatteförfarandelagen 39 kap. and facilitates checks that subcontractors have F-tax registration and employer contributions paid.

Sources: Skatteförfarandelagen 39 kap. · Byggföretagen

What responsibility does the building owner have for the work environment?

Under the Work Environment Act 3 kap. 6 § and AFS 1999:3, the building owner has overall responsibility for the work environment on the construction site. This includes appointing BAS-P and BAS-U, ensuring a work environment plan exists, notifying the project when required, and ensuring the coordinators have authority and resources. The responsibility cannot be contracted away, but certain tasks can be delegated in writing.

Sources: Arbetsmiljölagen 3 kap. 6-7 §§ · AFS 1999:3

What do you do in case of a workplace accident on the site?

In case of a serious workplace accident, the employer must under the Work Environment Act 3 kap. 3a § without delay report it to Arbetsmiljöverket via anmalarbetsskada.se. A serious accident includes death, serious personal injury or an incident that could have led to such. The accident site may not be disturbed until Arbetsmiljöverket permits it. Försäkringskassan also receives the report. Zaragoza AB has routines for incident reporting.

Sources: Arbetsmiljölagen 3 kap. 3a § · AFS 1977:1

What requirements apply to personal protective equipment (PPE)?

Under AFS 2001:3, the employer must provide personal protective equipment suitable for the risks, at no cost. On construction sites, helmet, S3 safety shoes, high-visibility jacket class 2 and safety goggles are basic equipment. Ear defenders are required at noise above 80 dB(A). The employer must train workers in use and maintenance. Damaged equipment must be replaced immediately.

Sources: AFS 2001:3 · AFS 2005:16

What is a safety representative and when is one required?

A safety representative is appointed under the Work Environment Act 6 kap. by the employees (via the union) at workplaces with at least 5 employees. On construction sites, regional safety representatives can also operate. The representative has the right to participate in work environment work, receive training, stop hazardous work under 6 kap. 7 §, and call for action from the employer and Arbetsmiljöverket.

Sources: Arbetsmiljölagen 6 kap. · AFS 2001:1

What rules apply to excavation and trench work?

Excavation work is governed by AFS 1981:15 (amended by AFS 2007:1). Excavations deeper than 1.25 m require sloping, shoring or other protection against collapse. Over 2 m, a work environment plan is always required. People may not be in an excavation without protection. Utility lines must be mapped before digging via Ledningskollen. Escape routes must be provided every 30 m in deep excavations.

Sources: AFS 1981:15 · AFS 1999:3 Bilaga A

What applies to solo work on a construction site?

Solo work is governed by AFS 1982:3. A risk assessment must be made, and particularly hazardous work (roof work, excavation work, hot work) may not be carried out alone. Alarm systems, regular contact or a GPS solution may be required. For people under 18, solo work with specific risks is prohibited. The employer must document routines for solo work in their systematic work environment management.

Sources: AFS 1982:3 · AFS 2001:1
