Solar panels & heat pumps

7 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 7 common questions and answers in the category solar panels & heat pumps for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

Is a building permit required for solar panels?

Since 1 August 2018, solar panels that follow the building's form are exempt from permit on one- and two-dwelling houses, unless placed on or within an area of cultural-historic value. Outside detaljplan and within certain areas, a building permit may still be required under PBL 9 kap. 3 c §.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 3 c § · Boverket

What is the green technology deduction?

The green technology deduction gives a tax reduction of 20% on material and labour for solar panels (maximum reduction 50,000 SEK per person per year), plus 50% for charging points and battery storage. It is administered via an invoice model where the installer deducts the amount directly.

Sources: Inkomstskattelagen 67 kap. · Skatteverket

Who is allowed to install solar panels?

The electrical installation must be carried out by an electrical installation company registered with Elsäkerhetsverket and with an authorised electrical installer. Panel mounting itself can be done by a builder, but the electrical connection must be performed by an authorised company under Elsäkerhetslagen 2016:732.

Sources: Elsäkerhetslagen 2016:732 · Elsäkerhetsverket

Is a notification required for an air heat pump?

An air-to-air heat pump normally requires neither a building permit nor a notification, but the outdoor unit may require a permit if it substantially alters the façade. An air-to-water heat pump affecting installations often requires a notification under PBF 6 kap. 5 §.

Sources: PBF 6 kap. 5 § · Boverket

How large a roof is needed for a solar panel installation?

An average villa needs 25–40 m² of roof for a 5–7 kW installation covering roughly half of its electricity use. South-facing gives the highest output, but east and west also work. Zaragoza AB offers a free roof analysis before solar installation.

Sources: Energimyndigheten

How large is the ROT deduction on heat pumps?

ROT gives a 30% reduction on labour up to 75,000 SEK per person per year (from 2024). Material costs are not included. ROT does not apply simultaneously with the green technology deduction for the same measure.

Sources: Inkomstskattelagen 67 kap. · Skatteverket

Can I sell surplus electricity from solar panels?

Yes, private individuals with microproduction (fuse max 63 A and feed-in max 43.5 kW) get a tax reduction of 60 öre/kWh for surplus electricity fed into the grid, up to 30,000 kWh per year. The electricity supplier additionally pays the market price for the electricity.

Sources: Inkomstskattelagen 67 kap. 27–33 §§ · Skatteverket
