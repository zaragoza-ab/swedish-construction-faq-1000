Fireplaces & wood burning

7 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 7 common questions and answers in the category fireplaces & wood burning for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

Does installing a stove require a building permit?

Installation of a fireplace or flue requires a permit-exempt notification to the building committee per PBF 6 kap. 5 §. A start notice is required before installation begins and a final notice once the check is complete. Zaragoza AB handles the notification and inspection plan.

Sources: PBF 6 kap. 5 § · BBR 5:4

What is the legal requirement on chimney sweeping?

Under Lag 2003:778 on protection against accidents, sweeping (cleaning) and fire-safety inspection must be carried out at intervals determined by the municipality. The municipality is responsible for sweeping, but the property owner may — under certain conditions — sweep themselves.

Sources: Lag 2003:778 · MSB

How often should a fire-safety inspection be done?

Per MSBFS 2014:6, fire-safety inspection of fireplaces and flues must be carried out every three or six years depending on the extent of use. The inspection is performed by the municipality's procured chimney-sweep technician and documented in a protocol.

Sources: MSBFS 2014:6 · MSB

What requirements apply to newly installed wood boilers?

Per BBR 6:741, newly installed wood boilers must be environmentally approved per EN 303-5 class 5 and fitted with an accumulator tank to reduce emissions. Emission limits for particulates and OGC have been tightened from 2022 under the Ecodesign Directive.

Sources: BBR 6:741 · Ekodesignförordning 2015/1189

Can I burn in an open fireplace in a residential area?

Yes, but the municipality may, through local health-protection regulations, restrict wood burning in densely built-up areas. Upon complaint, the environmental committee can intervene per Miljöbalken 9 kap. 3 § if the burning causes a nuisance to human health.

Sources: Miljöbalken 9 kap. 3 §

What are the requirements on flue distance to combustibles?

BBR 5:4241 and the manufacturer's installation instructions specify the clearance between a flue and combustible material. Normally at least 50–100 mm of free air or approved insulation is required. Incorrect distance is the most common cause of chimney fires.

Sources: BBR 5:4241 · MSB

Is terraced-house adaptation required for a shared chimney?

With a shared flue in a terraced house, each apartment's fireplace must be connected via its own channel to prevent the spread of smoke and fire. A fire-class EI 60 requirement for flues between apartments applies per BBR 5:533 and Boverket's general advice.

Sources: BBR 5:533 · Boverket
