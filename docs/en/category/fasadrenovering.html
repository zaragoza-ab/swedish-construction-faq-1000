Facade renovation

12 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 12 common questions and answers in the category facade renovation for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

Do I need a building permit for facade renovation?

Replacing with the same colour and material: usually no permit required. Changing appearance (colour, material, window size): a permit is required under PBL. In detailed development plan areas the rules are often strict.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 2 §

What does a facade renovation cost?

Rendered facade 600-1,200 SEK/m2, wooden panel 500-1,100 SEK/m2, brick 1,200-2,200 SEK/m2 (2026). 150 m2 of facade: 90,000-200,000 SEK labour plus materials.

Sources: Branschindex 2026

How often does a facade need repainting?

Wooden panel: 6-12 years depending on quality and weather. Rendered facade: 15-25 years. Brick less often, mainly repointing every 20-30 years. Zaragoza AB offers a 5-year warranty on completed facade work.

Sources: Branschpraxis

Does facade work qualify for ROT?

Yes, facade renovation, facade painting, insulation and window replacement all qualify for ROT on the labour cost.

Sources: Inkomstskattelagen 67 kap.

What is external facade insulation?

External insulation (usually mineral wool or EPS) with a new finish applied on the outside. Reduces heat loss by 20-40%. Often requires a building permit since the building becomes larger. Eligible for ROT.

Sources: BBR 9

How long does a facade renovation take?

Medium-sized house (150 m2 of facade): 2-4 weeks depending on material. Wood panel is faster, render takes longer (drying time between layers). Rain and cold extend the timeline. Optimal window: May-September.

Sources: Zaragoza AB erfarenhet

What is a rendered facade?

Render is a cement- or lime-based finish applied in several layers over masonry or insulation. Provides a smooth or textured appearance. Common in Skåne. Requires maintenance every 15-25 years.

Sources: AMA Hus

Can I change the colour of the facade?

Often yes, but in detailed development plan areas or cultural heritage zones a colour change can require a building permit. The City of Helsingborg has guidelines for older districts. Check with the Stadsbyggnadsförvaltningen (city planning office).

Sources: Helsingborgs stad

What is the difference between Weber and KC-bruk?

KC-bruk (lime-cement) is the traditional Swedish render mix. Weber is a branded product with modern performance mixes (strength, vapour openness). Both work; Weber is more expensive but more predictable.

Sources: Materialtillverkare

Can I combine facade renovation with window replacement?

Recommended. Window replacement is easier during facade work (scaffolding is in place, openings can be adjusted). Good economic synergy. ROT applies to both.

Sources: Branschpraxis

How do I tell if the facade has moisture damage?

Signs: flaking render, discoloured patches, salt efflorescence, soft/crumbly render when tapped. Indoors: mould, odour, stains near external walls. Zaragoza AB offers a free facade inspection in Helsingborg.

Sources: Fuktkontrollcentrum

Should I choose wood or render for the facade?

Wood: traditional, warm appearance, maintenance every 6-12 years. Render: modern/classic, 15-25 years between maintenance, better fire resistance. Skåne has a tradition of both. The choice depends on the house, neighbours and budget.

Sources: Arkitektur Sverige
