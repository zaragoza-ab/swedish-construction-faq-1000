Insurance & liability

7 fact-grounded Q&A · 2026-04-17 · CC BY 4.0

Here are 7 common questions and answers in the category insurance & liability for the Swedish construction industry. Every answer is anchored in primary law (PBL, BBR, KTjL, AFS, ABS 18, AB 04) and authoritative guidance. The data is open and free to use with attribution.

What does the building owner's responsibility mean?

The building owner has overall responsibility for ensuring that the construction work meets PBL, BBR and other regulations. The building owner must appoint a controlling officer (kontrollansvarig), establish a control plan, engage competent contractors and ensure that start and final approval decisions are obtained under PBL 10 kap.

Sources: PBL 10 kap. 5 § · PBL 10 kap. 9 §

What is a contractor's liability insurance?

Liability insurance protects the contractor against claims from third parties or the client for personal injury or property damage arising from the construction work. AB 04 kap. 5 § 23 requires the contractor to hold liability insurance with at least certain minimum amounts throughout the contracting and warranty period.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 5 § 23 · ABT 06 kap. 5 § 23

What does all-risk insurance cover in a construction contract?

All-risk insurance (entreprenadförsäkring) covers sudden and unforeseen physical damage to the works during construction, such as fire, theft, water damage and storm damage. Deductibles and exclusions vary. Under AB 04 kap. 5 § 22 the contractor must take out all-risk insurance unless the client does.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 5 § 22 · ABT 06 kap. 5 § 22

Is completion protection required for consumer construction contracts?

Yes, under the Act on Completion Protection (2014:227), completion protection (färdigställandeskydd) is required for single-family home contracts with a consumer if the contract sum exceeds the price base amount. The protection must cover the costs of completing the work if the contractor goes bankrupt or fails to fulfil its obligations.

Sources: Lag 2014:227 om färdigställandeskydd

Who is responsible for health and safety on the construction site?

The building owner bears the main responsibility and must appoint a construction work environment coordinator for planning (BAS-P) and for execution (BAS-U) under the Work Environment Act 3 kap. 6 § and AFS 1999:3. Each contractor is responsible for its own employees' work environment.

Sources: Arbetsmiljölagen 3 kap. 6 § · AFS 1999:3

How long does the contractor's liability last after final inspection?

Under AB 04 kap. 5 § 6 the warranty period is normally five years for the work and two years for materials. During the ten-year liability period, the contractor is liable for material defects caused by negligence under AB 04 kap. 5 § 7. Zaragoza AB follows AB 04 and ABT 06 in all construction contracts.

Sources: AB 04 kap. 5 § 6–7 · ABT 06 kap. 5 § 6–7

What is construction defect insurance?

Construction defect insurance (byggfelsförsäkring) was previously mandatory for single-family home construction, but the requirement was removed in 2014 and replaced by completion protection. Voluntary construction defect insurance can still be taken out and covers material construction defects for up to ten years after final inspection.

Sources: Lag 2014:227 · Boverket
