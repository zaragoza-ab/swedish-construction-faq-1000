Land & demolition permits

What is marklov (ground-works permit)?

Marklov is a permit required within a detailed development plan area to substantially change ground level, for example through excavation or fill. It may also be required for tree felling or forest planting if the detailed plan says so. Application is made to the municipal building committee under PBL 9 kap. 11–13 §§.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 11–13 §§ · Boverket

When is a demolition permit (rivningslov) required?

A demolition permit is required to demolish a building or part of one within a detailed development plan area. Outside a detailed plan, a notification may be enough. The building committee examines whether the building has cultural-historical value before granting the permit. Zaragoza AB handles the demolition notification and inspection plan per PBL.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 10 § · PBL 10 kap. 6 §

What is the difference between a building permit and a notification?

A building permit is a full regulatory decision required for new builds, extensions and major changes. A notification is a simpler procedure for measures such as attefall houses, fireplaces, ventilation or load-bearing structures. Both require a start notice before work may begin per PBL.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. · PBF 6 kap. 5 §

What should a demolition plan contain?

A demolition plan must describe which materials will arise, how hazardous waste (asbestos, PCB, mercury) is handled, recycling and transport to an approved recipient. The plan is part of the inspection plan and must be approved by the building committee before a start notice is given.

Sources: PBL 10 kap. 6 § · Avfallsförordningen 2020:614

Do I need a marklov to lower the plot by 0.5 metres?

Within a detailed development plan area a marklov is normally required if ground level changes by more than the plan allows, often for changes exceeding 0.5 metres. Check with the municipal building committee which limits apply in your detailed plan. Outside a detailed plan, marklov is rarely required.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 11 § · Boverket

How long is the processing time for a demolition permit?

The building committee must decide within ten weeks of receiving a complete application. The time may be extended by another ten weeks if needed. If the municipality misses the deadline, the fee is reduced per PBL 9 kap. 27 §.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 27 §

Is a demolition notification required for a friggebod?

No, friggebods under 15 m² are exempt from building permit and normally need no demolition notification. For attefall houses (30 m²), a notification is required both for construction and demolition if they were erected via notification. Always check with the municipality.

Sources: PBL 9 kap. 4–4c §§
