Reverse VAT

What is reverse charge VAT (omvänd skattskyldighet)?

Reverse-charge VAT means the buyer, not the seller, accounts for the VAT. Applies to the sale of construction services between two construction companies in Sweden. Introduced in 2007 to combat VAT fraud.

Sources: Mervärdesskattelagen 1 kap. 2 §

When does reverse-charge VAT apply?

Three conditions: 1) Swedish seller, 2) buyer is a construction company, 3) the service is a construction service. All three met → reverse charge. B2B only — never applies to private individuals.

Sources: ML 1 kap. 2 §

How do I write an invoice with reverse-charge VAT?

Invoice without VAT, with the text: "Omvänd skattskyldighet, byggtjänst — köparen redovisar momsen. 1 kap. 2 § 4 b ML". If the text is missing → Skatteverket may demand the seller pay the VAT themselves.

Sources: ML 11 kap. 8 §

Does reverse-charge VAT apply to materials?

No, pure materials sales without a service carry standard VAT of 25%. If the materials are integrated into the service (e.g. installation), the whole thing counts as a construction service → reverse charge on the total.

Sources: Skatteverkets ställningstagande

Does reverse-charge VAT apply to private customers?

No, never. Reverse-charge VAT only applies B2B between construction companies. Private customers are always charged standard VAT of 25%.

Sources: ML 1 kap. 2 §

What happens if reverse-charge VAT is applied incorrectly?

The seller becomes liable for the VAT plus interest. If the seller omits the text on the invoice, Skatteverket may require the seller to collect the VAT from the customer themselves. Complex situation — consult an accounting specialist.

Sources: Skatteverket

How is reverse-charge VAT reported in the tax return?

Seller: box 35 (sales subject to reverse charge). Buyer: box 24 (purchases), box 48 (output VAT) and box 49 (input VAT to deduct). Net effect is neutral with full right of deduction.

Sources: Skatteverket

What counts as a 'construction service' under the law?

Work on buildings, groundworks, installations (plumbing, electrical). Not: pure materials, design work (architect), equipment rental without an operator. Borderline: construction cleaning (reverse charge if part of a building project).

Sources: ML 1 kap. 2 §
